Prompt:

"Create a comprehensive, actionable 100-year strategy and implementation plan for a sports-betting analysis and parlay/ticket-generation application whose core requirement is to continuously maintain a predictive/competitive edge and to scale safely and profitably over decades. The product is an analysis/education tool (not a sportsbook); users place wagers elsewhere. Deliver a strategic roadmap, technical architecture, R&D plan, operational playbooks, governance, legal/compliance roadmap, commercial strategy, and monitoring/KPI framework that cover near-term (0–2 years), mid-term (3–10 years), long-term (10–30 years), and ultra-long-term (30–100 years) horizons.



Goals & constraints to assume

- Primary goal: sustain an actionable edge (better-than-market expected value) for users while ensuring the business remains profitable and compliant.

- Constraints: limited initial compute/budget; must respect privacy/regulation (GDPR/CCPA/etc.); must avoid facilitating illegal gambling; ethical/responsible-gambling obligations.

- Assume markets evolve: new models, privacy regimes, platform policy changes, adversarial actors, and competitor tactics.



Required deliverables (detailed)

1. Strategic thesis & value proposition

   - How the app will maintain an edge over time: unique data, model diversity, human-in-the-loop, proprietary features, partnerships, network effects, continual validation.

   - Core defensibility pillars and failure modes.



2. Multi-horizon roadmap

   - Near-term (0–2y): MVP improvements, data ingestion, baseline ML models, instrumentation, legal/age gating, pilot markets, pricing experiments.

   - Mid-term (3–10y): model specialization, proprietary data partnerships, vertical expansion, scalable infra, automated feature-gating, enterprise offerings, diversification of revenue.

   - Long-term (10–30y): persistent R&D org, model marketplace, decentralized data collaboratives/clean-rooms, global regulatory compliance program, capital reserves and hedging strategies.

   - Ultra-long-term (30–100y): institutionalization (endowment/treasury), governance for stewardship, succession planning, research incubators, adaptation to paradigm shifts (quantum, AGI, privacy-first compute).



3. Technical architecture & scalability plan

   - Data & feature strategy: private and public data sources, labeling, augmentation, continuous backtesting pipelines, metadata lineage, data contracts.

   - Model strategy: ensemble architectures, online learning, uncertainty quantification, domain adaptation, counterfactual simulation, continual retraining cadence, model evaluation metrics tied to economic edge.

   - Infrastructure: cloud & edge strategies, cost projections, autoscaling, multi-region deployment, disaster recovery, reproducibility (data + code lineage).

   - API & platform: rate-limited, tenant isolation, feature flags, A/B experimentation platform, SDKs.

   - Security & adversarial defenses: model theft prevention, poisoning detection, anomaly detection, robust encryption, credential handling.



4. Research & innovation program

   - Ongoing R&D org structure, research roadmap (statistical models, causal inference, simulation engines), partnerships with academia and sportsbooks (where legal), data acquisition plan, prize/challenge programs to discover talent/algorithms.

   - Open/closed research balance and IP strategy.



5. Data & partnerships strategy

   - Sources to prioritize (official sportsbooks feeds, betting exchanges, player/team data, tracking/sensor data, contextual news, sentiment), commercial partnership types, clean-room rules, data licensing and ethical use policies.

   - Data governance, retention, provenance, and privacy-preserving joins.



6. Commercial & product strategy

   - Monetization evolution: freemium → subscriptions → pay-per-ticket → enterprise/licensing → marketplace/aggregator fees.

   - Customer segments & go-to-market playbooks (retail bettors, syndicates, content creators, media, sportsbooks as partners).

   - Pricing frameworks and experiments tied to edge ROI for users (e.g., expected-value uplift pricing).



7. Compliance, legal & ethical program

   - Global regulatory roadmap, age/geo gating, advertising rules, affiliate disclosure rules.

   - Responsible-gambling integrations, opt-out/self-exclusion enforcement, harm-minimization design.

   - Auditability, model explainability requirements, recordkeeping for regulators.



8. Financial & risk management

   - Unit-economics guardrails, scenario-based financial projections (bull/baseline/bear), hedging/capital-reserve strategies, pricing guardrails to avoid negative unit economics.

   - Risk register: legal, reputational, model failure, data poisoning, competitor undercutting, platform delisting.



9. Organizational & governance plan

   - Org structure for product/engineering/research/legal/growth/ops.

   - Long-term governance: board, advisory, ethical review board, succession/sustainability planning, endowment or trust options for continuity.

   - Hiring roadmap and talent-retention strategies.



10. Monitoring, KPIs & feedback loops

    - Business KPIs: CAC, LTV, ARPU, churn, margin by cohort, revenue per ticket, ROI uplift for users.

    - Model KPIs: hit rate, calibration, expected value realized, concept drift metrics, backtest P&L, adversarial-detection stats.

    - Operational alerts and escalation playbooks.



11. Anti-competitive & resilience tactics

    - Defend against copycats: product velocity, continuous improvement, exclusive data/partnerships, user community/network effects.

    - Plan for platform/policy disruptions: contingency markets, alternative channels, decentralization strategies.



12. Milestones, timelines & investment asks

    - Concrete milestones for each horizon with measurable success criteria and estimated budget/staffing.

    - Suggested capital allocation across R&D, data acquisition, infra, legal/compliance, and growth.



13. Execution playbooks & templates

    - Example SOPs: new-data onboarding, model deployment & rollback, incident response, legal review for campaigns, partner onboarding checklist.

    - Example experiment playbook (design → exposure → metrics → decide).



14. Exit & continuity scenarios

    - Acquisition, IPO, partial monetization/licensing, or stewarded independence via foundation/trust; decision criteria and preparation steps.



15. Deliverables & formats

    - Executive summary, multi-horizon roadmap, architecture diagrams (text OK), data schema examples, model-evaluation dashboard specs, KPI dashboards, SOP templates, milestone Gantt, and financial scenario spreadsheet (CSV or table).

    - A prioritized 24-month tactical plan with tasks, owners, and estimated costs.



Required constraints for the AI to respect in output

- Emphasize ethical and legal compliance over pure revenue growth.

- No instructions that facilitate wrongdoing (illegal gambling facilitation, evading age/geo restrictions).

- Design all targeting/partnering to require explicit consent and privacy protections.



Questions you must ask me before producing the plan

- Desired monetization model(s) to prioritize.

- Appetite for partnerships with sportsbooks or data vendors.

- Risk tolerance for aggressive growth vs conservative, compliance-first approach.



Deliver the final plan as editable artifacts: a 2–3 page executive summary, detailed roadmap (CSV or numbered timeline), architecture & data-flow diagrams (text or Mermaid), KPI/dashboard templates, SOP/checklist templates, and the 24-month tactical plan with owners and cost estimates."
