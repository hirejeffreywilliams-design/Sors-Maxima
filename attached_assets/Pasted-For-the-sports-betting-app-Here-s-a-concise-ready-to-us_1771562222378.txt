"Create a daily parlay strategy for the user’s incoming games today. Inputs: `sport`, `date`, `games` (list with teams, start time, odds, injuries/notes), `bankroll`, `risk_level` (conservative/moderate/aggressive), `max_parlay_legs` (2–6), `preferred_bet_types` (moneyline/spread/total/team props/teaser), `max_tickets` (number of parlays to propose), `diversify` (yes/no). Output must include:

1. A one-paragraph overall strategy summary (budget, risk approach).

2. A prioritized list of recommended parlays (each parlay: legs, reason per leg, combined odds, suggested stake, expected payout, confidence score 1–5).

3. Suggested single bets to hedge or build bankroll (if any).

4. Bankroll allocation plan across proposed tickets (dollar amounts and % of bankroll).

5. Timing notes (when to place, key news to watch).

6. Quick risk reminder and recommended max loss for the day.



Follow these rules:

- Keep parlays within `max_parlay_legs`.

- For `conservative` favor favorites and 2–3 leg parlays; for `aggressive` include underdogs and up to `max_parlay_legs`.

- Include rationale for each leg (stats, matchup, injuries, odds value).

- Do not encourage reckless gambling; include responsible-gambling reminder."

Got it — you mean “crack the code” as in discover a repeatable, scalable playbook to drive distribution. Here’s a concise, practical process to do exactly that.



1) Clarify the north star

- Pick one leading metric (e.g., weekly active users, paid conversions, MRR). This is the signal you’ll optimize.



2) Diagnose current funnel

- Map funnel: awareness → acquisition → activation → retention → monetization.

- Instrument events and cohorts for each step (track conversion rates, CAC, LTV, churn).



3) Form hypotheses (repeatable format)

- Use a simple hypothesis template: “If we [change X], then [metric Y] will improve by Z% in N days because [reason].”

- Generate 10–20 hypotheses across acquisition, activation, retention, referral, and pricing.



4) Prioritize experiments

- Score by impact × confidence ÷ effort (ICE). Run highest-score experiments first.

- Limit to 1–3 parallel experiments per team to preserve learning clarity.



5) Run rapid, measurable experiments

- Define success criteria and sample size before starting.

- Use A/B tests or time-boxed pilots. Run long enough for statistical reliability or qualitative signal.

- Track leading and lagging indicators.



6) Capture learnings into a playbook

- For every experiment record: hypothesis, implementation, results (metrics + qualitative), why it worked/didn’t, next steps.

- Convert winners into standardized, repeatable workflows (creative templates, targeting recipes, onboarding copy, integration steps).



7) Build scalable channels & levers

- Focus on 2–3 highest ROI channels revealed by experiments. Treat others as test beds.

- For each channel document: audience, creatives, funnel flow, targeting, bid strategy, CAC, expected LTV.

- Automate repeatable tasks: onboarding sequences, email cadences, creative templates, integration scripts.



8) Create compounding mechanics

- Embed viral/referral loops, integrations, or content engines that scale without linear spend.

- Bake retention into product so acquisition investments compound (e.g., collaboration features that invite others).



9) Optimize unit economics

- Ensure CAC < allowed payback period (e.g., < 6–12 months) and LTV/CAC target (e.g., > 3 for SaaS).

- If not, optimize onboarding, pricing, monetization tiers, and highest-value cohorts.



10) Scale with guardrails

- As you scale spending, keep experiments running to avoid saturation.

- Monitor cohort retention changes and channel decay; cap spend where ROI declines.

- Institutionalize weekly growth reviews and a prioritized experiment backlog.



11) Institutionalize learning & hiring

- Create a central growth playbook and dashboard.

- Hire or train for roles: growth PM, data analyst, creative lead, partnerships/BD.

- Regular retrospectives to evolve playbook.



Quick 90-day execution template

- Weeks 1–2: Instrument funnel, define north star, generate/prioritize hypotheses.

- Weeks 3–6: Run top 4 experiments (across acquisition + activation). Measure & document.

- Weeks 7–10: Scale 1–2 winners, start building automation & compounding mechanics.

- Weeks 11–12: Reassess unit economics, expand channel budget, hire/assign roles as needed.
