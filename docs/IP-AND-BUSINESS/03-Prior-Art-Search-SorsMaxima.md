## ACADEMIC LITERATURE REVIEW

### Sports Prediction AI Literature

**Dixon & Coles (1997), "Modelling Association Football Scores":** Foundational academic paper on statistical soccer prediction. Uses bivariate Poisson model. Addresses a single sport with a limited factor set (attack/defense strength). Does not address market dynamics, psychological factors, or Monte Carlo simulation.

**Hvattum & Arntzen (2010), "Using ELO Ratings for Match Result Prediction":** ELO rating adaptation for sports prediction. Single-factor model (relative strength). Not a multi-factor fusion engine, no market intelligence integration.

**Constantinou et al. (2012), "Towards Smart-Data: Improving Predictive Accuracy in Long-term Football Matches":** Bayesian network for soccer prediction. Addresses several factors but does not include market dynamics factors, sharp money detection, or Monte Carlo simulation.

**Beal et al. (2019), "Artificial Intelligence for Team Sports: A Survey":** Survey of AI approaches in team sports. Documents the state of the art — confirms that multi-factor fusion engines with market intelligence integration, autonomous learning, and Monte Carlo simulation at the scale of the present invention are not documented in the academic literature.

**Kovalchik (2016), "Searching for the GOAT of Tennis Win Prediction":** Tennis win prediction model comparison. Single sport, no market intelligence, no Monte Carlo.

### Brier Score in Sports Prediction Literature

Academic use of Brier Score for sports prediction calibration exists but is confined to academic evaluation contexts. No prior art was identified using Brier Score as an operational calibration metric within a production platform that triggers automated recalibration events — the specific implementation of the present invention's Calibration Engine.

### Monte Carlo Sports Simulation Academic Literature

Academic Monte Carlo simulation for sports prediction typically uses 1,000–10,000 simulations for academic evaluation purposes, not as a production real-time operational system. The implementation of 100,000-iteration nightly deep simulation as an operational production feature is not documented in prior academic literature.
