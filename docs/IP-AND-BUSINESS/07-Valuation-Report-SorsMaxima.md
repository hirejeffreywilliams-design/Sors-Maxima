## KEY VALUE DRIVERS

### Driver 1: The Quantum Fusion Engine — Proprietary Architecture

The 46-factor Quantum Fusion Engine with patent-pending protection represents a uniquely defensible technical asset. The specific combination of 46 factors across 8 categories, Monte Carlo integration at scale, and Autonomous Learning creates a prediction system that competitors cannot easily replicate — even if they knew the factor list, the calibrated weights embedded in the trained model through 987+ settled picks represent years of machine learning investment.

### Driver 2: The Autonomous Learning Compounding Advantage

Every settled pick improves the Quantum Fusion Engine. At 987+ picks, the model is well-trained. At 5,000 picks (Year 2), it will be significantly more accurate. At 25,000 picks (Year 3), the calibration advantage over a newly launched competitor will be enormous. This compounding learning advantage creates an exponentially growing competitive moat that is time-valued — the longer the platform operates, the more valuable the training dataset becomes.

### Driver 3: The Market Timing

Post-PASPA, the US legal sports betting market is in the fastest growth phase of its history. Platforms that establish brand recognition and user loyalty during this growth phase will capture disproportionate long-term market share. Sors Maxima is deploying its 229,746-line production platform at the optimal moment in market development.

### Driver 4: The Sports Betting Media Acquisition Premium

Sports betting information companies have attracted significant M&A activity. The New York Times acquired The Athletic for $550M. Better Collective acquired Action Network for $240M. Genius Sports acquired Second Spectrum for $200M. Acquirers are paying 8–15x ARR for quality sports intelligence assets. At even $5M ARR (achievable by Year 2–3), Sors Maxima at a 10x multiple would imply $50M — well above the current pre-revenue valuation range.

### Driver 5: API Licensing Optionality

The Quantum Fusion Engine can be licensed as an API to sports media companies, sportsbooks, and content creators who want AI pick capabilities without building their own prediction engine. This B2B licensing optionality represents a high-margin revenue stream not fully captured in consumer subscription projections.
